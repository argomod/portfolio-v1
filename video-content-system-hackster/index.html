To display Hackster's Facebook & YouTube videos on our own website, I designed a video upload system & a new card for the home page.

UX/UI

The viewing experience

Because different videos are hosted on Facebook and YouTube, the design needed to be platform-independent.

Working with the dev team to understand their limits in respect to our deadline, I opted to use an embed system for displaying the videos – this allowed us to keep users on our site without needing to host videos ourselves.

The embed type changes to autoplay only on desktop-sized screens. A dark, cinematic background fills the screen with a title, author, and description below.

Desktop mockup of YouTube video embed

Building a backend

The biggest challenge was building an easy to use backend for uploading and displaying video content. I opted to split the system in two: one for storing videos and one for displaying videos. Hackster envisioned using video content for future products, so having a single database to select from eliminated redundancy.

The first system I designed keeps a database of all the videos Hackster will want to use. Each video is uploaded with a link to YouTube or Facebook, basic information, and tags for future use – just in case.

The second system allows admins to select videos to display in a featured videos section. Items can be selected, rearranged, and edited swiftly. I also included a button to take admins to the first system just in case they forgot to upload a video (a popular feature).

Displaying videos

I designed a new card to show the videos on our home page. The style matches the existing cards used for other types of content. A play button indicates the content is a video and the sizing aligns with a 12 column grid.
