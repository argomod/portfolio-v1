I redesigned Hackster’s signup form to improve conversion rates & provide a better first impression. I also improved the login experience along the way.

UX/UI, graphic design

Gathering insights

When considering the process of acquiring a new user to our website, we realized the signup form plays a crucial role – not just in converting users, but also in making a good first impression. User data revealed several shortcomings of our previous design. While the majority our users signed up with social logins, those were not the first options presented.

We also found many accounts with only one login attempt after creation. That directed our attention toward error flows, which in our previous design were unspecific. And lastly, we discovered a low rate of onboarding completion. Because onboarding happens directly after signing up, I saw this as an opportunity to build motivation early on.

I collaborated with the dev and marketing teams to determine which fields were absolutely necessary, reducing them down to just two. While ideating, I decided to include the login experience with the signup experience. This is because many errors relate to the other flow, such as a user signing up with an email they’ve already signed up with or a user logging in when they haven’t registered yet. Each field is also fully validated with their own error messages.

Error validation is underrated – each of these sample screens has a unique message identifying the specific issue

Designing a signup popup that pops

The design for the new signup form not only needed to adhere to UI standards, but also provide a positive impression of what Hackster is. I opted for a modal window that expands full-screen on mobile. I designed the vector graphic with existing assets from our fantastic in-house artist and wrote copy based on data of users’ favorite parts of the site.
